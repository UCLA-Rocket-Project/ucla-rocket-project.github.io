Project Overview

Ares is our flagship liquid bi-propellant rocket team. The goal of the project is to cultivate experienced rocket engineers through the design, build, and test of a liquid bi-propellant rocket to 45,000 feet. Our rocket's architecture allows the project to be based around an attainable 1-year deliverable at incredibly low cost for a liquid system. Beyond this, everything within reason is built in house to maximize educational value.

Outcomes

In its first 4 years, Project Ares has launched 4 liquid powered rockets and conducted over 14 liquid static fires.

UCLA now holds the record for the highest student-built liquid bi-propellant rocket flown. Our rocket Tranquility, launched in April 2019, lost telemetry connection at about 14,600 feet while traveling at a speed of Mach 1.3. Our rocket Ambition, launched in May 2021, beat our own record and successfully launched and recovered to an altitude of 22,000 feet. For 3 years running, UCLA has been the only school to launch a rocket at the FAR-Mars liquid rocket competition.

We aim to reach higher altitudes with each year and design iteration, staying at the forefront of collegiate rocketry.
